Our Story

About the School

Baba Fateh Singh Ji Punjabi/Gurmat School is located at Keysborough Gurdwara Sahib. Classes are run every Sunday, free of cost, by dedicated volunteers from the Sikh community.

Our school offers a warm, nurturing environment where children learn Punjabi language, Gurmat principles, and Kirtan. We believe in preserving our heritage and passing it on to the next generation.

The school is named after Sahibzada Baba Fateh Singh Ji, the youngest son of Guru Gobind Singh Ji, who demonstrated extraordinary courage and steadfast faith at just 6 years of age. His spirit of bravery and devotion inspires everything we do.

Sahibzada Fateh Singh Ji was born in Anandpur Sahib on 12 December 1699. In December 1705, along with his brother Sahibzada Zorawar Singh Ji, he was presented before Nawab Wazir Khan of Sirhind and offered safe passage if he converted to Islam. Both Sahibzaday refused with a courage that belied their years. They were martyred, bricked up alive within a wall — their sacrifice remembered for all time at Gurdwara Fatehgarh Sahib.

We honour their memory by educating the next generation in the language, values, and spirit of Sikhi.

What We Offer

Our Programs

We offer three programs at Keysborough Gurdwara Sahib. Punjabi and Gurmat are completely free. Kirtan is available at a nominal cost through dedicated music teachers.

📚
Punjabi

Learn to read, write and speak Punjabi using the Gurmukhi script. Classes run across 6 levels from beginner to advanced.

📅 Every Sunday 💛 Free of charge 👥 Classes 1A, 1B, 2, 3A, 3B, 3C, 4, 5, 6
🙏
Gurmat

Study Sikh history, Gurbani, and Sikh values. Students learn about our Gurus, Sikh traditions, and the principles of Sikhi.

📅 Every Sunday 💛 Free of charge 👥 All ages welcome
🎵
Kirtan

Sacred Sikh devotional music — learn to sing and play Gurbani kirtan. Taught by dedicated music teachers.

📅 Every Sunday 💲 Nominal fee applies 👥 All ages welcome
